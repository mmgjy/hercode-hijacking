# Challenge Brief

## Mission

Build a reusable system that detects emerging retail opportunities from global and local market signals.

Use a Swiss outdoor retailer as the starter case. Your system should identify promising outdoor-retail opportunities, explain where each opportunity appears first, assess whether it can transfer into Switzerland or DACH, and recommend what the retailer should do next.

## What Counts As An Opportunity

An opportunity can be a:

- Product type.
- Product feature or material.
- Usage occasion.
- Service or rental model.
- Merchandising idea.
- Brand or supplier lead.
- Price or assortment gap.
- Content or community angle.

## Recommended Flow

1. Gather market signals from several source types.
2. Normalize the evidence into structured rows.
3. Deduplicate repeated signals across markets and sources.
4. Score each candidate opportunity.
5. Assess Swiss/DACH transferability.
6. Convert the best candidates into business actions.
7. Show the result in a dashboard, report, API, notebook, or other demo-ready format.

## Quality Bar

For each recommended opportunity, preserve:

- Source names and URLs.
- Market or geography.
- Why it is emerging now.
- Signal strength and limitations.
- Transferability reasoning for Switzerland or DACH.
- Recommended next action.
- Confidence, risks, and missing evidence.

Separate directional signals from hard commercial proof. Weak evidence should trigger more research rather than overconfident recommendations.
